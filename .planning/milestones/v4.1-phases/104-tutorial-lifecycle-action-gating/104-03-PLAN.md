<objective>
Expose auto-fill suppression as a real, tested substrate capability (hard-constraint #5): a tutorial
step that wants the learner to perform the click (Phase 109's teaching beat) must be able to suppress
the single-enabled-choice auto-fill that would otherwise rob the interaction.

Purpose: Target gating to a single allowed choice triggers `tryAutoFillSelection` auto-resolution
(useActionController.ts:481-500). That is the DESIRABLE default â€” but the substrate must NOT hardcode
auto-fill on. This plan wires the step's `suppressAutoFill` signal into the auto-fill guard and proves
both the default (auto-fill resolves) and the suppressed (teaching click preserved) paths.
Output: a guard in `tryAutoFillSelection` and extended `useActionController.test.ts` coverage.
</objective>

tryAutoFillSelection (src/ui/composables/useActionController.ts:481-500):
- Early-returns if `!getAutoFill() || isExecuting.value`.
- Filters `choices.filter(c => !c.disabled)`; auto-fills when exactly one enabled choice remains and the
  selection is not optional, then recurses to the next selection via fetchAndAutoFill (:506-525).

getAutoFill (useActionController.ts:482): a controller-level option, default ON. `autoFill: false` is the
existing suppression knob. The tutorial signal must reach this guard PER TAUGHT SELECTION (a step can teach
one selection while auto-filling others), not just globally.

Tutorial step signal (from Plan 104-01): `TutorialStep.suppressAutoFill?: boolean`, surfaced to the client
on `PlayerGameState.tutorial` (active step). The composable reads the active-step + its `suppressAutoFill`
(and, if the step targets a specific selection name, only suppress that selection). This is a UNIT change;
the test can construct the controller state directly without the session projection.
